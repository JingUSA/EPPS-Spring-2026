Text Analytics using quanteda

1. Read tutorial of quanteda

The quanteda package is an R package for text analysis. Its basic workflow is:

  • corpus
  • tokens
  • document-feature matrix (dfm)
  • text statistics and models

It can be used for tokenization, word frequencies, document similarity, and text scaling methods such as Wordfish.

Interpretation

The inaugural speeches show clear similarities because many presidents repeatedly emphasize themes such as freedom, people, government, union, and America. At the same time, there are also important differences across time and among presidents. Speeches from wartime or crisis periods tend to place more emphasis on conflict, peace, or security, while speeches in other periods may focus more on democracy, unity, or government reform.

  1. What is Wordfish?

Wordfish is an unsupervised text scaling model. It estimates document positions on an underlying latent dimension using word frequencies. In this assignment, that latent dimension can be interpreted as differences in strategic framing or emphasis across DOD reports.

  1. How to compare positions

There are at least two ways to compare document positions.

First, we can use the Wordfish theta estimates. Documents with similar theta values are closer in language and framing, while documents with very different theta values are farther apart.
